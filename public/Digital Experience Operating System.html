Foreword
From Marketing Function to Revenue Operating System

Most organisations treat Digital Experience as a marketing execution function. That is a structural mistake. At Career360, we are building something categorically different — a Revenue Operating System (RevOS) that connects every piece of content, every user interaction, every lead, and every data point into a closed-loop intelligence engine.

This document is the complete blueprint. It covers every layer — from the L0 enterprise strategy down to L4 individual role execution, from high-level UCC dashboard architecture to field-level data dictionary definitions. Use it to align your team, build systems, govern processes, and scale globally.

Core Principle
"Content = Demand Generation Asset · Data = Strategic Advantage · UX = Conversion Multiplier · Automation = Cost Efficiency · Analytics = Decision Engine"
Chapter 01

Overview & Strategic Vision

The Digital Experience Command Centre (DXCC) is Career360's centralised value-creation engine — directly driving pipeline velocity, CAC efficiency, brand equity, and conversion yield across the entire ecosystem.

What is DigitalExp?

DigitalExp is not a marketing team. It is a Revenue Production System — a centralised shared-services command unit that sits at the intersection of Content, Technology, Data, and Commercial Operations. It serves every entity in the Career360 ecosystem: Career360Abroad, Career360Academy, euRecruiter, Altus Career, and myUniApply.

Strategic Definition
"DigitalExp is a Predictive Revenue Intelligence System where every content piece is a measurable asset, every user is a tracked journey, and every interaction is a monetisable signal."

Data Architecture, ERD & Event Dictionary

The single source of truth model across the Career360 ecosystem — standardised entities, relationships, field-level data dictionary, and the 100+ event taxonomy that powers the UCC intelligence layer.

Critical Warning

"If data is not standardised, your AI, dashboards, and decisions will fail." Every field must be defined, validated, owned, and governed.

Core Data Entities — ERD Overview

PERSON
person_id · name · phone · email · country · persona_type (Student/Parent/Agent)
LEAD
lead_id · person_id(FK) · source_channel · campaign_id · program_interest · status · lead_score · created_at
CAMPAIGN
campaign_id · channel · budget · CPL · start_date · end_date · objective · ROI
CONTENT
content_id · type · topic · platform · persona · funnel_stage · publish_date · engagement_score
CONVERSION
conversion_id · lead_id · amount · program · date · channel_attribution · counsellor_id
EVENT LOG ⭐
event_id · event_name · person_id · system_source · timestamp · funnel_stage · metadata (JSON)

Relationship Logic

  • Person → Lead (1:Many) — one person can have multiple lead records
  • Campaign → Lead (1:Many) — one campaign generates many leads
  • Content → Interaction (1:Many) — one content piece has many user interactions
  • Lead → Conversion (1:1 or 1:Many) — a lead may convert to multiple programs
  • All Entities → Event Log (centralised) — every action emits an event

Data Governance Rules

🎯 Single Source of Truth
No data must exist in two places without a master record. SSOT must be defined per entity and documented.
🔍 Deduplication Engine
No duplicate leads allowed. Deduplication by phone + email + program combination before CRM entry.
✅ Mandatory Validation
All mandatory fields validated at point of entry. Invalid records quarantined, not stored.
👤 Data Ownership
Every data field has a named owner responsible for quality, accuracy, and timeliness.
📋 Audit Logs
All data changes logged with timestamp, user ID, before/after values. ISO 27001 compliant.
🔒 Access Control
Role-based access — no user sees data outside their defined scope. RBAC enforced at API level.

Lead Management SOP — Trigger: Lead Created
1
Auto Lead Capture via CRM
Form submission → CRM entry auto-created · KPI: Form → CRM sync rate 100%
2
Lead Scoring Applied
AI model scores lead 0-100 based on program fit, intent signals, engagement history · KPI: Score accuracy
3
Lead Assigned Within SLA (5 Minutes)
Rule-based assignment by program, language, geography · KPI: Assignment within SLA %
4
WhatsApp + Email Triggered Automatically
Personalised first-touch message with program info + appointment link · KPI: Open rate, Response rate
5
Appointment Scheduled
Calendly/CRM appointment link + reminder sequence · KPI: Appointment show rate
6
Counselling → Eligibility → Status Updated
Structured call → eligibility check → CRM status updated → Revenue recorded · KPI: Conversion rate

SOP B: Campaign Execution

Step 1: Campaign Brief
Define objective, target persona, geography, budget, timeline, success KPIs
Step 2: Creative Development
Script → Design → Video → A/B variants → Internal review → Approval
Step 3: Platform Setup
Audience targeting, pixel placement, UTM tracking, bid strategy, frequency caps
Step 4: Launch + Monitor Daily
Daily CTR, CPL, ROAS review. Pause underperformers within 24hrs of detection.
Step 5: Optimise
Creative rotation, audience refresh, budget reallocation to best-performing adsets
Step 6: Report + Archive
Campaign post-mortem report → Store in DAM → Feed learnings into next brief
